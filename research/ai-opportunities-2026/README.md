# AI Opportunity Knowledge Base — 2026-06-29

This folder is the starting knowledge base for opportunistic AI trends as of **2026-06-29**. It is built for action, not generic trend watching: each opportunity should connect to a buyer, pain, MVP wedge, risks, evidence, and a next decision.

## Executive readout

The most opportunistic 2026 AI trend is not “more AI adoption.” Adoption is already broad. The gap is **safe production value capture**: companies are experimenting with AI and agents, but they are struggling with workflow redesign, data readiness, governance, security, integration, and measurable ROI.

Savant’s strongest positioning: **secure, governed AI systems that actually ship.**

## What the evidence says

- **AI is broadly adopted but not broadly scaled.** McKinsey’s 2025 global survey: 88% regular AI use in at least one business function; only about one-third scaling across the enterprise; 39% report enterprise-level EBIT impact.
- **Agent demand is ahead of operating maturity.** McKinsey: 62% experimenting with agents, 23% scaling at least one agentic system somewhere; Gartner forecasts rapid app-level agent adoption but also high project cancellation risk when ROI/governance are weak.
- **Trust/governance is now implementation work.** McKinsey’s 2026 AI trust survey emphasizes that agentic systems can misuse tools or take unintended actions; responsible AI maturity lags in governance and agentic controls.
- **Regulation creates a dated buying trigger.** EU AI Act rules apply progressively; the majority of rules and enforcement start **2026-08-02** per the European Commission AI Act Service Desk.
- **Capability is commoditizing; context and execution matter.** Stanford HAI’s 2026 AI Index reports rapid technical progress, broad adoption, and a “jagged frontier” where agents still fail meaningful task benchmarks.
- **Engineering teams need operating models, not just tools.** Gartner says coding agents are now orchestrating SDLC workflows and pricing is shifting toward usage-based consumption, creating governance/cost/eval needs.
- **SMBs are large enough to matter and moving fast.** SBA Office of Advocacy says there are 36.2M U.S. small businesses employing 62.3M people; U.S. Chamber reports 58% of small businesses use generative AI, up from 40% in 2024.
- **Routine clerical/admin work is under pressure across sectors.** WEF’s 2025 Future of Jobs report expects AI/information processing to transform 86% of businesses by 2030 and flags clerical/admin roles among the declining categories.

## Evidence standard for future KB updates

Do not add broad “AI is growing” claims unless they change a build/sell decision. Every opportunity update should include:

- Source link and access date.
- Buyer and decision-maker.
- Pain frequency and cost.
- Existing alternatives.
- Integration requirements.
- Legal/security constraints.
- Testable MVP hypothesis.
- Kill criteria.
