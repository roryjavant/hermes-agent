# O5 — Professional Services Operations Automation

## Buyer and decision-maker

- **Primary buyer:** law firm owner, managing partner, accounting/tax firm owner, agency owner, consulting-firm operations lead.
- **Decision-maker:** owner/operator or managing partner with authority over practice-management systems and billing workflows.

## Pain and why now

Professional services firms sell expertise but lose margin to intake, document handling, time capture, billing, follow-up, and fragmented client communications. Legal-industry reports show measurable operational pain: 8am/MyCase report that firms using streamlined workflows can recover 15+ hours/month, 61% report greater efficiency with AI-powered billing/invoicing, and passive time tracking can recapture up to $4,000/month in missed billables.

Vendor survey data is not independent market proof, but it is strong buyer-language evidence: firms care about collections, billable capture, errors, deadlines, and client communications.

## MVP wedge

**Professional Services Ops Sprint:** automate one back-office bottleneck without touching professional judgment.

Candidate workflows:

1. Intake summarization and conflict/checklist routing.
2. Document request follow-up.
3. Meeting/call summary to task list.
4. Passive time-capture review queue.
5. Billing narrative cleanup.
6. Client status update drafts.
7. Deadline/reminder dashboards.

## Integration/data requirements

- Practice-management/CRM/accounting system access or exports.
- Sample matters/projects with redacted data.
- Clear boundaries between admin drafting and professional advice.

## Legal/security/compliance constraints

- Avoid legal, financial, or tax advice generation unless reviewed by a licensed professional.
- Keep humans responsible for final client-facing outputs.
- Log drafts, approvals, and source documents.
- Respect confidentiality and privilege/work-product boundaries for law firms.

## Proof/KPI

- Hours recovered per month.
- Billing cycle time.
- Missed follow-ups/deadlines.
- Billable capture.
- Client response time.
- Reduction in admin rework.

## Kill criteria

- No access to workflow system.
- Buyer wants AI to independently give professional advice.
- No measurable baseline.
- Firm cannot agree on confidentiality/data handling.
