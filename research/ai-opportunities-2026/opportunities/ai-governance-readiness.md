# O2 — AI Governance and Readiness

## Buyer and decision-maker

- **Primary buyer:** founder, COO, CTO, CISO, compliance lead, operations leader, board-facing executive.
- **Decision-maker:** executive who owns risk, procurement, or AI rollout.
- **Best-fit organizations:** teams using AI informally, regulated companies, firms with EU exposure, and companies preparing to deploy agentic workflows.

## Pain and why now

The opportunity is not policy writing for its own sake. The buying trigger is operational: teams are already using AI, but they do not know what is approved, what data is entering tools, how outputs are checked, or how to produce evidence for customers, auditors, insurers, and regulators.

Evidence signals:

- NIST AI RMF gives U.S. organizations a practical voluntary framework for trustworthy AI risk management.
- EU AI Act obligations are rolling in through 2027; even U.S. companies with EU customers may face buyer due-diligence pressure.
- U.S. Chamber reports small businesses worry that fragmented state AI/privacy laws could increase compliance burden and slow AI adoption.

## MVP wedge

**AI Governance Readiness Audit:** a 1–2 week implementation-readiness engagement.

Deliverables:

1. AI use-case inventory.
2. Vendor/tool inventory.
3. Data classification by use case.
4. Risk tiering.
5. Human approval and escalation map.
6. Logging/evidence requirements.
7. Policy starter kit.
8. 30/60/90-day controls roadmap.

## Integration/data requirements

- Interviews with business, IT, security, and operations stakeholders.
- Existing vendor list and procurement/security questionnaires if available.
- Sample workflows and data categories; no need for raw sensitive data during audit.

## Legal/security/compliance constraints

- Position as technical readiness and implementation support, **not legal advice**.
- Recommend counsel review for regulated/high-risk uses.
- Include vendor data-use, retention, access, and auditability questions.
- Treat agentic systems as higher-risk when they can use tools, trigger external actions, or write to business systems.

## Proof/KPI

- Inventory completeness.
- Number of high-risk uses discovered.
- Number of shadow-AI tools retired, approved, or controlled.
- Procurement/security unblock.
- Controls adopted within 30 days.

## Kill criteria

- Buyer only wants a formal legal opinion.
- No executive owner.
- No access to current AI use cases.
- No intent to implement controls after the assessment.
